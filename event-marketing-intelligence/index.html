Event Marketing Intelligence

B2B event marketing is a high-stakes, low-data game. You're committing $20K–$200K based on attendee estimates and sales pitches from conference organizers. KeynoteData gives you actual intelligence, speaker rosters, sponsor history, and session data, before you write the check.

The problem with conference decisions today

Most B2B event marketing decisions follow the same broken process:

  • A conference rep sends you a prospectus with impressive attendee numbers
  • You check with your team about whether they've heard of the event
  • You look at last year's speaker page (if it's still up)
  • You decide based on gut feel and relationship with the rep

The result: you spend $50K at a conference and spend three days talking to vendors instead of buyers. Or you skip an event that your top three prospects all attended.

KeynoteData gives you a different starting point: actual data on who speaks, who sponsors, and what gets discussed at the conferences you're considering.

What intelligent event marketing looks like

Before committing to a conference, data-driven event marketers ask:

  • What's the seniority mix of speakers? (Lots of practitioners, few executives = different ROI than an executive-heavy event)
  • Which companies are in the speaker lineup? (Your target accounts speaking = their teams attend)
  • Who sponsors this event? (Sponsors reveal who else is chasing this audience)
  • What topics dominate the sessions? (Session themes reveal audience interests and maturity)
  • Is this a one-and-done sponsor or a conference with repeat commitment from brands like yours?

KeynoteData answers these questions with actual data, not estimates.
